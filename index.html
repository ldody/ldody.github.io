Framatome

Sensitivity analysis of fuel assemblies in nuclear power plant cores

Non-disclosure agreement: no data or results available

‹

Source: FRAMATOME, framatome.com

  1. Context:

    2. FA (Fuel Assemblies) in nuclear power plant core are subjected to mechanical and thermohydraulic constraints leading to lateral deformations. Excessive deformations in extrem conditions could prevent the fission control and then create safety hazards. Those deformations are calculated and simulated for each creep step with a MM (Main Model) coupling mechanical calculations using Python and hydraulic calculations using STARCCM+. In order to reduce the processing time and limit the license use of STARCCM+, RM (Reduced Model) was developed for FA deformations calculations.

  2. Goal:

    3. The purpose of this internship was to validate the RM for FA deformations simulation with RM and perform sensitivity analysis. And then to carry out UQ (Uncertainty Quantification) in order to determine the most sensitive parameters on deformations and severity index (FA bow-based index). It involves a lot of machine learning and data analysis.

  3. Reduced model validation:

    4. To validate the RM for simulation and sensitivity analysis, processing time and accuracy were compared with the MM. Fifties calculations for different FA designs and reactor powers were performed on Linux server.
    The processing time was determined by recovering the creation dates of calculation step files with a Python script and then plotted on a time-step graph.
    The RM accuracy was determined by plotting deformations and severity index distributions according to the FA location and creep step with Python script. Then, the results are compared with MM to calculate the error between the two models and check they are following the same distribution trend. Deformations and severity index are plotted in this kind of core matrix according to their FA location.
    The processing time and accuracy comparisons allow to validate the RM to perform simulation and sensitivity analysis on FA deformations.

  4. Uncertainty quantification:

    5. UQ allows to determine inputs influence on outputs. Different methods exist:
    • Local analysis method: fastest method consisting in modifying input parameters one by one in order to determine the most influential ones.
    • Global analysis method: slowest method consisting in modifying every parameter at the same time to determine the most influential ones according to their own sensitivity and their interactions with the others.

    OAT: local sensitivity analysis

    The OAT (One-At-a-Time) sensitivity analysis which is a local sensitivity method was first used to obtain a first approach of the most influential parameters (e.g. creep time, diameter) among a library used by the RM.
    Hundreds of calculations were performed for different reactor powers with different parameter values. Those calculations were automatically performed with a Python script that modify the library, launch the calculations and post-treat the results by plotting the severity index in function of parameter value with a standardized abscissa and calculating their slope coefficients.
    Those results allow to choose the parameters that will be used for global sensitivity analysis according to the analysis objectives as model uncertainties or product development.

    PCE: global sensitivity analysis

    PCE (Polynomial Chaos Expansion) is a method to perform global sensitivity analysis. it consists in decomposing the output into function of polynomials according to input parameters.
    `R = \sum_(i=1)^p \alpha_i \Psi_i(\xi)`
    Then the calculation of Sobol's indices give the most influential parameters. This is a variance-based decomposition method that gives two indices:
    • The main Sobol's index gives the influence of a single parameter independently of the others. Its formula is:
      • `S_i = (V[E[Y|X_i]])/(V(Y))`
    • The total Sobol's index gives the influence of a single parameters taking in account its interactions with the others. Its formula is:
      • `S_i^T = 1-(V[E[Y|X_(~i)]])/(V(Y))`
    The calculations were performed using Dakota software coupled with Python script.
    Here is the kind of results obtained after post-processing (random values are used). According to the main Sobol's indices, p3 and p4 are the most sensitive parameters. Despite the fact that p4 main Sobol's index is higher than p3, its interactions with other parameters are less sensitive than p3 because its total Sobol's index is lower. The other parameters have a low global sensitivity according to total Sobol's indices. Their sensitivity is mainly local.
    The most sensitive parameters are determined and the simulation results can be analyzed to caracterise the evolution direction of results according to inputs with a parallel coordinates plot.
    In the end of this internship, the most sensitive parameters and their influence on results evolution were identified throught hundreds of calculations for different FA designs, reactor powers and sensibility objectives. Allowing to quantify model uncertainties and product improvments.

Thales

Development of an aircraft defense system

Non-disclosure agreement: no data or results available

‹

Source: THALES, thalesgroup.com

  1. Goal:

    2. The purpose of this internship was to develop an entire innovative concept of an aircraft defense system. This concept involved aerodynamics, electronics, pyrotechnics and mechanics, from functional specifications to mechanical design.

  2. Operational use and technical specifications:

    3. I defined the operational use according to the environment in which the system will evolve and its purposes. From this, it is possible to define the technical specifications with a FAST (Function Analysis System Technic) chart that the product must meet.

    Source: Value Analysis Canada, valueanalysis.ca

  3. System architecture:

    4. The system architecture is composed of 4 parts: electronics, pyrotechnics, aerodynamics and mechanics.

    Electronics and pyrotechnics (internal components):

    I defined the internal components of the aircraft around which the structure will be built. For the electronics, I made a list of required components (e.g. camera) and their connections between each other. It allowed me to select the components according to the technical specifications and quantify the total weight and power needed to operate them. For the pyrotechnics, I used the technologies already provided by the company.

    Aerodynamics and mechanics:

    Once the internal components defined, it is possible to determine the required propulsion system and flight technologies to make the aircraft operational. Firstly, I chose the propulsion system according to technical specifications and benefits provided. Then, I was able to use the equations of flight mechanics to determine different characteristics of the product as wing area, aircraft shape.

    Source: Researchgate, researchgate.net

    The aerodynamics forces are:
    • The drag is an opposed force to the movement of a body in a flow.
      • `D = 1/2 \rho A V^2 C_D`
    • The lift is a force perpendicular to the movement direction of a flow.
      • `L = 1/2 \rho A V^2 C_L`
    In the image above, these forces are combined with incidence angle to characterize different flight steps. But other angles exist to make a turn by creating centrifugal forces. This will not be explained in detailed.
    As it is a concept study, the drag and lift coefficients are determined from abacuses according to different shapes. These calculations allowed me to create operating ranges charts (e.g. speed, power, autonomy) with Excel according to the different variables as drag and lift coefficients or wing area. Here is a kind of charts plotted (random values are used).

  4. Mechanical structure:

    5. The previous section allowed me to size the different components of the aircraft. First, I designed the internal components with Creo then I set them up according to their connections and location in the aircraft. Thus, I was able to design the mechanical structure around by meeting the previous results and the technical specifications.
    At the end, I delivered a concept study of a makeable and operational of an aircraft defense system.
